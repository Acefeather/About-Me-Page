About Me

I'm a recent high school graduate and first year Cybersecurity and Dance student at BYU. In my free time, I love to play video games, play the cello, and binge movies and tv shows. I have three sisters (my poor dad, I know), and a little dog named Winston. When deciding what I wanted to major in in college, I found myself entirely split between my two passions: Cybersecurity and Modern Dance. Despite their differences, I found a love for what each could offer me separately and decided to pursue a double major in both.

Education

I graduated from Central Valley High School. My passion for technology started at a young age and has only grown since then. Last year marked the beginning of my journey with Cybersecurity under the insruction of Joseph Pauley. He introduced me to the introductory concepts of Cybersecurity and Networking and encouraged me to continue pursing this field after high school.

I also dedicated 15 years to my dance training at Isabelle's Dance Time and in various summer intensives in the Western U.S., including Ballet West Academy, State Street Ballet, and Gonzaga University's summer dance programs. These experiences not only prepared me for living independently from my parents, but taught me invaluable skills in cooperation, perseverence, and even the positive impact that rest can have on one's progress.

Experience

Both competitions offered pressure and a responsibility to my teammates that motivated me and taught me both how to rely on my abilities and to recognize when I needed help.

Through the Sisters in Stem club at CVHS, I was also able to travel to nearby middle schools and educate young girls on the opportunities their are for women in STEM.
